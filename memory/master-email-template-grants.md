# MASTER TEMPLATE - Grant/Project Opportunity Sharing

**Use this for EVERY grant/project opportunity. Assume team knows nothing.**

---

## EMAIL SUBJECT LINE
`[OPPORTUNITY] [Program Name] - [Location] - Deadline: [Date]`

---

## OPENING LINE

Dear [Name],

We have a [xxx] EUR value opportunity we should explore.

---

## SECTION 1: THE 5 W's + H

### WHAT
[One clear sentence describing the opportunity]

### WHERE
[Geographic location]

### WHEN
- **Application Deadline:** [Date]
- **Internal Go/No-Go Decision:** [Date - typically 7 days before deadline]
- **Project Start (if approved):** [Date]
- **Project Duration:** [X months/years]

### WHO (Key Players)

| Role | Organization | Contact Person | Email/Phone | What They Do |
|------|-------------|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| Applicant | [Who submits the grant] | [Name] | [Contact] | Owns application, manages funds |
| Our Role | [Zero-X / EX Venture / MFC] | [Name] | [Contact] | What we provide |
| Partner 1 | [Organization] | [Name] | [Email] | [Their contribution] |
| Partner 2 | [Organization] | [Name] | [Email] | [Their contribution] |
| Funder | [Government/Agency] | [Department] | [Contact] | Provides grant money |

### WHY (Why This Matters)
- [Strategic reason 1 - market opportunity]
- [Strategic reason 2 - validation/pilot value]
- [Strategic reason 3 - relationship building]

### HOW MUCH (Budget Structure)
- **Total Project Size:** [Describe scale without specific euro amounts]
- **Grant Structure:** [Matching vs non-matching]
- **Budget Categories:**
  - Equipment/Vendor: [Who receives this]
  - Research/Studies: [Who receives this]
  - Training/Capacity: [Who receives this]
  - Management/Admin: [Who receives this]

## SECTION 2: WHAT YOU NEED TO DO

**Your Tasks This Week [Role/Name]:**
- [ ] Task 1: [Specific action] (Deadline: [Date])
- [ ] Task 2: [Specific action] (Deadline: [Date])
- [ ] Report back: [Format and deadline]

---

## SECTION 3: REQUIREMENTS & DECISION CRITERIA

### Must Have (Proceed Only If All Met)
- [ ] [Requirement 1 - e.g., partner confirms availability]
- [ ] [Requirement 2 - e.g., technical feasibility confirmed]
- [ ] [Requirement 3 - e.g., internal approval obtained]

### Nice to Have (Strengthens Application)
- [ ] [Optional item 1]
- [ ] [Optional item 2]

### Key Risks
- [Risk 1]
- [Risk 2]

---

## SECTION 4: DECISION FRAMEWORK

### PROCEED If:
- All "Must Have" requirements met by [date]
- No major blockers identified
- Team confirms capacity to execute without distraction from priorities

### ABORT If:
- Any "Must Have" requirement fails
- Partners unresponsive by [date]
- Technical incompatibilities discovered
- Creates friction with Tier 1 priorities (CCM Canaries, Seraph Australia)
- Any dependency stalls beyond deadline

---

## SECTION 5: REPORTING FORMAT

By [Deadline], reply with:
1. **Status:** Green (proceed) / Yellow (questions) / Red (abort)
2. **Requirements Status:** Which items met/not met
3. **Partner Responses:** Summary of what partners said
4. **Blockers:** Any issues preventing proceed
5. **Recommendation:** Continue or kill with brief rationale
6. **Next Steps:** If green, immediate next actions

---

## SECTION 6: REFERENCE INFORMATION

- **Program Website:** [URL]
- **Application Guidelines:** [URL]
- **Funder Contact:** [Email/Phone]
- **Internal Contact:** [Who to ask for clarification]

---

## SECTION 7: WHAT NOT TO DO

### DO NOT:
- :x: Share this email with external parties without approval
- :x: Include specific euro amounts (€480K, €960K, etc.) in external communications
- :x: Discuss budget breakdowns with partners until partnership confirmed
- :x: Commit to timelines or deliverables without Julien approval
- :x: Share information about other projects (CCM, Seraph, etc.) in this context
- :x: Negotiate financial terms with partners (escalate to Julien)
- :x: Spend time on grant writing if deal-breaker requirements not met
- :x: Miss the internal decision deadline (7 days before external deadline)
- :x: Escalate minor questions answered in guidelines
- :x: Share inter-company strategy (Dubai entity, extraction model, etc.)

### DO:
- :white_check_mark: Focus only on this opportunity (no mixing projects)
- :white_check_mark: Verify partner availability before any commitments
- :white_check_mark: Escalate immediately if technical showstoppers found
- :white_check_mark: Report "Red" status promptly if opportunity blocked
- :white_check_mark: Keep information within Zero-X team only (unless specified otherwise)
- :white_check_mark: Ask questions if anything unclear
- :white_check_mark: Abort quickly if friction detected

---

## SECTION 8: ESCALATION PROTOCOL

### Escalate to Julien Immediately:
- Any deal-breaker requirement blocked
- Partner negotiations stall or terms change
- Technical incompatibility discovered
- Funder changes scope or requirements
- Any issue not covered in this brief
- Questions about budget or financial terms

### Do NOT Escalate:
- Routine questions answered in guidelines
- Standard documentation requests
- Timeline clarifications
- Minor scope adjustments
- Template or formatting questions
