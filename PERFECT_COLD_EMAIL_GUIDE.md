## 1. SUBJECT LINE (Most Important)

### ✅ DO:
- Keep it under 50 characters
- Make it specific to their research/role
- Use their institution name when relevant
- Create curiosity without being clickbait
- Examples:
  - "Kollaborationsmöglichkeit – [Institution] – [Topic]"
  - "Research Partnership – [Specific Technology]"
  - "Syngas Integration – Follow-up from [Conference]"

### ❌ DON'T:
- Use ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation!!!
- Write generic subjects like "Hello" or "Partnership"
- Include power specs (50-500kW) in subject
- Use spam triggers: "Free", "Urgent", "Act Now", "Limited Time"
- Make it too long (gets truncated)

---

## 2. OPENING LINE (First 2 Sentences)

### ✅ DO:
- Show you know THEIR work specifically
- Mention a specific paper, project, or achievement
- Connect their expertise to your need
- Be brief - 1-2 sentences max
- Examples:
  - "I read your recent paper on [Topic] in [Journal] with great interest."
  - "Your work on [Specific Technology] at [Institution] aligns closely with our research."
  - "Following your presentation at [Conference], I wanted to reach out."

### ❌ DON'T:
- Start with "My name is..." (wastes prime real estate)
- Use generic flattery without specifics
- Open with "I hope this email finds you well" (overused)
- Make it about YOU first

---

## 3. BODY (The Pitch)

### ✅ DO:
- Keep total email under 150 words
- Lead with VALUE for them, not your needs
- Be specific about WHY them specifically
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Include the collaboration disclaimer: "We seek research partners, not customers"
- Mention funding opportunities if relevant (Eurostars, Horizon Europe)

### ❌ DON'T:
- Include power specs (50-500kW) - this turns academics off
- Use marketing language ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "disruptive")
- Write long paragraphs (walls of text)
- Focus on product features instead of research collaboration
- Include pricing or commercial details

---

## 4. CALL TO ACTION (CTA)

### ✅ DO:
- Ask for ONE specific thing
- Provide options (makes it easy to say yes)
- Give a clear next step
- Examples:
  - "Would you be available for a 30-minute call next week?"
  - "Are you open to discussing potential collaboration?"
  - "Would [Day] at [Time] or [Alternative Day] work for a brief discussion?"

### ❌ DON'T:
- Ask multiple things at once
- Be vague ("Let me know if you're interested")
- Demand immediate response
- Include more than one CTA
