## Country Intelligence

### Ghana (Priority 1 - Active)
**Electrification:**
- National: 85% (2022)
- Rural: 67%
- Grid reliability: Moderate (frequent outages)

**Energy Policy:**
- Renewable Energy Act 2011 (Act 832)
- Mini-grid licensing framework active
- Net metering available
- Rural electrification fund exists

**Feedstock Availability:**
- Palm waste (major producer)
- Cocoa husks (world's #2 producer)
- Rice straw (northern regions)
- Wood waste (sawmills)

**Key Players:**
- Regulator: Energy Commission
- Utility: ECG (Electricity Company of Ghana)
- Mini-grid developers: BBoxx, PEG Africa, SolarTurtle
- Development partners: GIZ, USAID, World Bank

**Active Opportunity:**
- Anaergia Africa - Ghana Golden Foods
- Contact: Alberto Borello
- Status: Introduction sent, awaiting follow-up
- Value: €1.2M
- Feedstock: Palm kernel shells, EFB

**Challenges:**
- Grid extension competing with off-grid
- Cedi volatility (GHS/EUR)
- Import duties on equipment

### Nigeria (Priority 2 - Large Market)
**Electrification:**
- National: 55% (2022)
- Rural: 39%
- Grid reliability: Poor (daily outages common)

**Energy Policy:**
- Mini-grid regulation: NERC (2016)
- Significant off-grid opportunity
- Feed-in tariff for renewables
- Rural electrification agency (REA)

**Feedstock Availability:**
- Rice husks (massive production)
- Cassava waste
- Sawdust (deforestation concerns)
- Oil palm waste (southern regions)

**Key Players:**
- Regulator: NERC
- Utility: Privatized DisCos (distribution companies)
- Mini-grid developers: Rensource, GVE Projects
- Development partners: USAID, AfDB, World Bank

**Opportunities:**
- Largest unelectrified population in Africa
- Strong agricultural base
- Significant diesel replacement market

**Challenges:**
- Security issues (north, Niger Delta)
- Complex regulatory environment
- Currency volatility (NGN)
- Infrastructure gaps

---

### Kenya (Priority 3 - Innovation Hub)
**Electrification:**
- National: 75% (2022)
- Rural: 65%
- Grid reliability: Moderate

**Energy Policy:**
- Leading mini-grid framework in Africa
- Feed-in tariff (FIT) for biomass
- Strong off-grid solar market
- Kenya Vision 2030 energy goals

**Feedstock Availability:**
- Sugarcane bagasse (western Kenya)
- Coffee husks (central highlands)
- Wood chips (sustainable forestry)
- Maize cobs (widespread)

**Key Players:**
- Regulator: EPRA
- Utility: Kenya Power
- Mini-grid developers: PowerGen, Rendeavour
- Development partners: GIZ, USAID, DFID

**Opportunities:**
- Innovation hub (Nairobi)
- Strong mobile money (M-Pesa)
- Supportive policy environment
- Active carbon credit market

**Challenges:**
- High solar penetration (competition)
- Grid expansion aggressive
- Land tenure complexities

## Market Trends

### Solar vs Biomass
- Solar dominates mini-grid narrative
- Biomass opportunity: Baseload power, waste utilization
- Hybrid systems gaining traction

### Carbon Credits
- Growing interest in African projects
- Verra and Gold Standard active
- CORSIA (aviation offset) potential
- Article 6 (Paris Agreement) implementation

### Productive Use
- Shift from basic electrification to economic development
- Agro-processing as anchor load
- Cold storage, milling, drying applications

## Key Learnings

### What Works
- Government partnership reduces risk
- Local EPC essential for maintenance
- Community engagement critical
- Feedstock security must be contractual
- Mobile money for PAYGO

### What Doesn't Work
- Importing Western models directly
- Underestimating logistics challenges
- Ignoring currency risk
- Overlooking maintenance capacity
- Assuming grid won't expand

### Common Mistakes
- Inadequate feedstock assessment
- Weak local partnership
- Unrealistic timeline expectations
- Insufficient community engagement
- Poor after-sales service planning
