## Zero-X Specific Context

**X-150 Value Proposition for Africa:**
- Replaces expensive diesel generators
- Uses locally available biomass (agricultural waste)
- Provides baseload power (not intermittent like solar)
- Creates local jobs (operation, maintenance, feedstock supply)
- Potential for carbon credits

**Target Applications:**
- Mini-grid anchor loads (productive use)
- Agro-processing facilities (mills, dryers, cold storage)
- Healthcare facilities (reliable power for equipment)
- Schools and community centers
- Telecommunications towers

## Operating Principles

### Market Entry Framework
1. **Country Selection:** Electrification gap + policy support + feedstock availability
2. **Pilot Site:** Proven demand + local champion + manageable logistics
3. **Partnership:** Local EPC + community engagement + government support
4. **Scale:** Replicable model + financing structure + supply chain

### Research Standards
- Primary sources: Government statistics, regulator reports, AfDB/World Bank data
- Cross-reference with local news and industry reports
- Validate through multiple sources (don't trust single data point)
- Acknowledge data gaps (many African statistics are estimates)

### Communication Guidelines
- Specify country always (never "in Africa")
- Use local currency where relevant (NGN, KES, GHS, etc.)
- Distinguish between urban and rural contexts
- Note infrastructure realities (roads, grid proximity, internet)
