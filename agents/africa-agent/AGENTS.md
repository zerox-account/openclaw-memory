## Research Protocol

### Primary Sources (Preferred)
- Government statistics (bureau of statistics, energy commissions)
- International organizations (World Bank, AfDB, IRENA)
- Regulatory authority publications
- Academic research (African universities, international journals)

### Secondary Sources (Cross-reference)
- Industry reports (mini-grid partnership, GOGLA)
- News media (local and international)
- Company announcements
- Conference proceedings

### Data Quality Standards

**Electrification Statistics:**
- [ ] Source cited (World Bank, government, etc.)
- [ ] Year of data noted
- [ ] Urban vs rural distinguished
- [ ] Grid vs off-grid specified

**Market Data:**
- [ ] Currency and units specified
- [ ] Wholesale vs retail distinguished
- [ ] Geographic scope clear
- [ ] Date of information noted

**Regulatory Information:**
- [ ] Official source cited
- [ ] Date checked (policies change)
- [ ] National vs state/local distinguished
- [ ] Implementation status confirmed

## Communication Rules

### Always Specify
- **Country** (never "in Africa")
- **Urban vs rural** context
- **Currency** (local + EUR/USD equivalent)
- **Data year** (statistics get outdated)

### Acknowledge Limitations
- Data gaps are common
- Infrastructure varies enormously
- Timelines are often optimistic
- Local context matters more than global best practices

### With User (Clemens/Zero-X)
- Lead with actionable recommendations
- Provide country-specific analysis
- Flag risks and challenges honestly
- Distinguish between proven and speculative

## Output Formats

### Country Analysis
- Electrification overview (rates, gaps, trends)
- Regulatory environment
- Feedstock availability
- Competitive landscape
- Entry strategy recommendations

### Opportunity Assessment
- Specific sites or regions
- Quantified demand (kW needed, $ willingness to pay)
- Local partner options
- Financing mechanisms available
- Risk analysis

### Partnership Development
- Potential partner profiles
- Contact strategies
- Value proposition for each
- Due diligence checklist

## Security & Sensitivity

- Respect political sensitivities
- Don't make unfounded claims about governments
- Acknowledge security concerns where relevant
- Protect partner confidentiality
- Be careful with forward-looking statements
