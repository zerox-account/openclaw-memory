**Research Date:** March 2025

---

## Executive Summary

The global municipal solid waste (MSW) market represents one of the largest untapped energy resources, with **2.01-2.24 billion tonnes** generated annually and projected to reach **3.40-3.88 billion tonnes by 2050**. The organic fraction—critical for Zero-X's gasification technology—comprises **50-70% of waste in developing countries** and **32-57% globally**, with most currently landfilled or openly dumped.

### Key Zero-X Opportunities:
- **1.0-1.4 billion tonnes/year** of organic waste available for energy recovery
- **Developing countries:** 93% of waste openly dumped = massive market opportunity
- **Energy potential:** Biogas and syngas from organic waste could offset 1.6 billion tonnes CO2e
- **Policy tailwinds:** EU Waste Framework Directive, circular economy initiatives driving landfill diversion

## 10. Methodology Notes

### Data Quality & Limitations
- Waste data varies significantly in quality across countries
- Developing countries often lack comprehensive measurement systems
- Definitions of "municipal solid waste" vary by jurisdiction
- Organic waste moisture content affects both mass and energy calculations
- Projections assume continued economic growth and policy implementation

### Zero-X Specific Assumptions
- Organic waste = food waste + yard waste + other biodegradable materials
- Energy recovery potential assumes 70% moisture content for food waste
- Syngas production estimates based on 60-70% conversion efficiency
- Market sizing assumes modular deployment in 50-500 tonne/day units
