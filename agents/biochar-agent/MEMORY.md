## Regulatory Landscape

### EU - European Biochar Certificate (EBC)
- **Authority:** European Biochar Industry (EBI)
- **Classes:** Basic, Agro, AgroOrganic
- **Key Requirements:** Heavy metals <limits, PAHs <limits, H:C ratio <0.7
- **Timeline:** 3-6 months
- **Cost:** €2,000-5,000 initial

### EU Carbon Removal Certification (CRC)
- **Status:** Framework under development (2024-2025)
- **Opportunity:** Biochar as permanent carbon removal
- **Price Expectation:** €50-150/tonne CO2

### US - IBI Certification
- **Authority:** International Biochar Initiative
- **Recognition:** Global
- **Timeline:** 2-4 months
- **Cost:** $1,500-3,000

## Zero-X Biochar Profile

**Expected Characteristics:**
- Source: Bio digester sludge gasification
- Process: 800-1000°C downdraft
- Ash content: High (30-50% expected)
- pH: Alkaline (9-11 expected)
- Carbon content: Moderate (30-50%)

**Applications Best Suited:**
- Soil amendment (pH correction)
- Compost additive
- Construction materials (high ash)
- Less suited: Premium horticulture (ash too high)

**Certification Path:**
- EBC-Basic: Achievable
- EBC-Agro: Possible with testing
- EBC-AgroOrganic: Unlikely (sludge origin)

## Key Learnings

### What Works
- EU market most accessible (proximity, regulations)
- Carbon credits add 20-40% value
- Bulk sales more profitable than retail
- Certification essential for premium pricing

### What Doesn't Work
- Organic certification for sludge biochar
- Retail markets without established brand
- Long-distance shipping for low-value product
- Competing on price alone

### Emerging Opportunities
- Japan premium market (high prices, quality focus)
- Carbon credit stacking (multiple standards)
- Construction materials (concrete additive)
- Water filtration (activated biochar)
