# Data Center Trigeneration (CHC) Market Research Report

**Prepared for:** Zero-X Labs GmbH  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Research Focus:** Global data center market opportunities for Combined Heat, Cooling, and Power (CHC) / Trigeneration systems

---

## Executive Summary

The global data center market is experiencing unprecedented growth driven by AI workloads, creating a critical inflection point for trigeneration (CHC) solutions. With average rack densities jumping from 16 kW to 27 kW (69% YoY increase) and facilities scaling to 38 MW average capacity, data centers face mounting pressure on power grids, cooling systems, and sustainability commitments. This creates a $600+ billion infrastructure opportunity where CHC systems can deliver compelling value through PUE improvement, diesel replacement, waste heat utilization, and carbon reduction.

**Key Market Indicators:**
- Global hyperscale data center capacity expected to double in just over 12 quarters
- Data centers projected to consume 9-17% of total US electricity by 2030 (up from 4-5% today)
- 36% of operators already deploying liquid cooling; 28% planning adoption within 12-24 months
- 25% of operators now using on-site power generation (up from 19% last year)
- Hyperscaler capex projected to exceed $600 billion in 2026

**Source:** AFCOM State of the Data Center Report 2026, EPRI "Powering Intelligence 2026"

### 1.2 Power Density Trends

The AI revolution is driving a step-function change in rack density:

- **2021:** 7 kW average per rack
- **2024:** 16 kW average per rack  
- **2025:** 27 kW average per rack (**69% YoY increase - largest ever recorded**)
- **2026+:** Nearly 70% of operators expect further increases within 12-36 months

**Implications for CHC:**
- Higher heat rejection requirements create demand for absorption cooling
- Concentrated thermal loads improve waste heat recovery economics
- Power infrastructure must scale proportionally

### 1.3 Cooling Requirements & PUE Targets

Current cooling landscape:
- **36%** of operators have deployed liquid cooling
- **28%** plan adoption within 12-24 months
- **40%** indicate current cooling solutions don't meet operational requirements
- **PUE targets:** Leading facilities achieving <1.2 (atNorth DEN01 with DLC)

**Cooling Technology Adoption:**
| Technology | Current Deployment | Planned (12-24 mo) |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Liquid Cooling | 36% | +28% |
| Direct Liquid Cooling (DLC) | Growing | High-density AI standard |
| Immersion Cooling | Early adoption | Emerging |
| Heat Pumps for Heat Reuse | Pilot stage | Accelerating |

### 1.4 Diesel Backup Dependency & Replacement Opportunity

**Current State:**
- Diesel generators remain standard for Tier III/IV facilities
- Growing regulatory pressure to reduce emissions
- Sustainability commitments driving alternative power searches
- Grid interconnection delays (2-5 years) forcing behind-the-meter solutions

**CHC Opportunity:**
- Replace diesel with natural gas/biogas CHC for primary backup
- Continuous operation capability vs. standby-only diesel
- Waste heat recovery improves overall system efficiency
- Carbon reduction aligns with sustainability goals

## 4. Trigeneration (CHC) Value Proposition

### 4.1 PUE Improvement Potential

**Current PUE Benchmarks:**
- Industry average: 1.5-1.6
- Best-in-class: 1.1-1.2
- NREL ESIF: 1.04 (with waste heat recovery)

**CHC Impact on PUE:**
| Component | Typical Contribution | CHC Improvement |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| IT Load | 1.0 (baseline) | 1.0 |
| Cooling | 0.3-0.4 | 0.1-0.2 (absorption) |
| Power Infrastructure | 0.1-0.15 | 0.05-0.08 (on-site) |
| **Total PUE** | **1.4-1.55** | **1.15-1.28** |

**Key Mechanisms:**
- Absorption chillers from waste heat reduce electrical cooling
- On-site generation eliminates transmission losses
- Heat recovery for reheat/dehumidification

### 4.2 Diesel Replacement Savings

**Cost Comparison (per MW of backup capacity):**

| Factor | Diesel Generators | CHC System |
|--------|------------------|------------|
| Capital Cost | $800-1,200/kW | $1,500-2,500/kW |
| Fuel Cost (annual) | $50-100/kW | $30-60/kW (natural gas) |
| Maintenance | $20-40/kW/year | $40-60/kW/year |
| Utilization | <1% (standby) | 50-90% (continuous) |
| **Value Add** | Backup only | Primary power + cooling |

**Additional Value:**
- Revenue from grid services (demand response)
- Carbon credit monetization
- Avoided diesel fuel storage/spill risks

### 4.3 Waste Heat Utilization for Cooling

**Technical Pathways:**

1. **Absorption Chillers**
   - Single-effect: COP 0.6-0.8
   - Double-effect: COP 1.0-1.2
   - Heat source: 80-120°C exhaust/chilled water

2. **Adsorption Chillers**
   - Lower heat requirements (60-80°C)
   - Suitable for data center waste heat temperatures

3. **Heat Pumps**
   - Temperature boost for district heating
   - COP 3-5 for heating applications

**Case Study - atNorth DEN01:**
- 22.5 MW capacity for high-density AI workloads
- Direct Liquid Cooling (DLC) technology
- PUE <1.2
- Heat pump integration with Vestforbrænding district heating
- Heating 8,000+ homes

### 4.4 Carbon Reduction for Sustainability Goals

**Hyperscaler Commitments:**
- Google: 24/7 carbon-free energy by 2030
- Microsoft: Carbon negative by 2030
- Amazon: Net-zero carbon by 2040
- Meta: Net-zero emissions across value chain by 2030

**CHC Carbon Impact:**
| Scenario | Emissions vs. Grid Average | vs. Diesel Backup |
