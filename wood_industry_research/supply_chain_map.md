## Regional Flow Analysis

### US Southeast (Georgia, Alabama, Mississippi, Louisiana)

**Waste Generation**
- ~25M tonnes/year sawmill residues
- ~10M tonnes/year forestry logging residues
- Peak: Softwood harvest season (fall/winter)

**Current Flows**
1. **Sawmill residues:**
   - 40% → Pellet plants (Enviva, Drax)
   - 35% → Pulp mills (declining capacity)
   - 20% → Boiler fuel (on-site)
   - 5% → Landfill (bark, fines)

2. **Forestry residues:**
   - 60% → Left in field (decomposition/burning)
   - 25% → Pulpwood (low grade)
   - 15% → Pellet raw material

**Gasification Opportunities**
- ✅ Sawmill on-site power/heat (replace diesel generators)
- ✅ Small-scale pellet plant syngas integration
- ⚠️  Forestry residue collection costs high ($30-40/tonne)
- ✅ C&D wood waste processing (contamination issues)

---

### Southeast Asia (Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand)

**Waste Generation**
- 32M tonnes/year EFB (Indonesia)
- 22M tonnes/year EFB (Malaysia)
- 6M tonnes/year EFB (Thailand)

**Current Flows**
1. **EFB (Empty Fruit Bunches):**
   - 60% → Mulch/compost (low value)
   - 25% → Incineration (open burning)
   - 10% → Pulp/paper trials (limited success)
   - 5% → Landfill

2. **Palm Kernel Shells:**
   - 70% → Export (Japan/Korea for power)
   - 25% → Mill boiler fuel
   - 5% → Charcoal

**Gasification Opportunities**
- ✅ EFB drying + gasification for mill power
- ✅ Combined EFB + PKS gasification (better ash profile)
- ✅ Biochar byproduct for plantation soil amendment
- ✅ Biogas upgrading from POME (synergy with gasification)

---

### Europe (Sweden, Finland, Baltics)

**Waste Generation**
- ~15M tonnes/year forestry residues
- Well-integrated existing bioenergy infrastructure

**Current Flows**
- 45% → District heating (CHP)
- 30% → Pellet production
- 20% → Pulp/paper industry
- 5% → Other

**Gasification Opportunities**
- ⚠️  Market saturated with existing solutions
- ✅ Methanol/syngas for transport fuels (BECCS angle)
- ✅ Hydrogen production via biomass gasification

## Identified Gaps (Gasification Opportunities)

### Gap 1: Sawmill On-Site Generation
- **Problem:** Sawmills burn diesel for backup power, pay grid premiums
- **Opportunity:** 1-5MW gasification units using sawdust/bark
- **Target:** 50+ sawmills in US Southeast

### Gap 2: EFB Valorization
- **Problem:** 60M tonnes/year EFB wasted or burned inefficiently
- **Opportunity:** Mill-integrated drying + gasification
- **Target:** Top 20 palm oil groups in SE Asia

### Gap 3: Pellet Plant Rejects
- **Problem:** 5-10% of material rejected from pellet lines (fines, high ash)
- **Opportunity:** On-site syngas for plant energy needs
- **Target:** All major pellet producers

### Gap 4: C&D Wood Waste
- **Problem:** Construction debris mostly landfilled (contamination)
- **Opportunity:** Sorting + gasification for RDF (refuse-derived fuel)
- **Target:** Urban waste processors

## Transportation Economics

| Mode | Distance | Cost ($/tonne) | Best For |
|------|----------|----------------|----------|
| Truck | 0-100 miles | $8-15 | Local mill waste |
| Truck | 100-300 miles | $20-35 | Regional aggregation |
| Rail | 300-1000 miles | $15-25 | Pellet plants |
| Barge | River access | $10-20 | Gulf Coast |
| Ocean | Export | $25-40 | Asia-Europe trade |

**Economic Radius for Gasification:**
- On-site: Always viable if waste disposal cost > $30/tonne
- Hub model: Max 50-mile radius for low-value feedstock
- Maximize: Integration with existing waste point
