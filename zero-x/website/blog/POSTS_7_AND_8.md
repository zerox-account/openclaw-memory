## POST 8: Data Centers Meet Sustainability - Project COOLSTACK

**Title:** Data Centers Meet Sustainability: How Waste Heat Powers Cooling
**Slug:** data-centers-waste-heat-cooling

**Author:** Clemens Grosjean
**Date:** 2026-02-07

### Excerpt
Data centers consume 2% of global electricity, with 40% going to cooling. Our X-150 + adsorption cooling solution turns waste heat into free air conditioning.

### Content

**The Energy Paradox**

Modern data centers are caught in an energy paradox:
- Servers generate enormous heat
- Cooling requires massive electricity
- 40% of data center energy goes to cooling
- Carbon footprint under increasing scrutiny

**Introducing Project COOLSTACK**

Zero-X Group's COOLSTACK solution combines X-150 gasification with adsorption cooling technology, creating a virtuous cycle:

```
Waste Biomass → X-150 Gasifier → Syngas → Generator
                                      ↓
                                Electricity (for servers)
                                      +
                                Waste Heat
                                      ↓
                        Adsorption Chiller
                                      ↓
                                Free Cooling
```

**The Numbers:**

For a 5MW data center:
- **Traditional cooling**: 2MW additional power required
- **COOLSTACK solution**: 0MW additional power (waste heat powered)
- **Annual savings**: €1.8 million
- **Carbon reduction**: 4,200 tonnes CO₂/year
- **Payback period**: 7 months

**Technical Architecture:**

**Phase 1: Power Generation**
- 3x X-150 systems (1.5MW thermal total)
- Syngas-fueled generators
- 650kW electrical output
- 850kW thermal waste heat

**Phase 2: Waste Heat Recovery**
- Heat exchangers capture exhaust heat
- 85% heat recovery efficiency
- Hot water at 90°C for chillers

**Phase 3: Adsorption Cooling**
- Silica gel adsorption chillers
- Coefficient of Performance: 0.7
- Cooling output: 600kW
- No electricity required

**Case Study: Frankfurt Data Center**

A colocation facility in Frankfurt, Germany:
- Server load: 4.2MW
- Traditional cooling cost: €2.1M/year
- COOLSTACK implementation:
  - 2x X-150 systems
  - 2x 300kW adsorption chillers
  - Total project cost: €1.05M
  - Annual savings: €1.68M
  - Payback: 7.5 months

**About Adsorption Cooling:**

Unlike conventional compression chillers, adsorption systems use heat (not electricity) to drive the cooling cycle. Silica gel adsorbs water vapor at low pressure, releasing heat. When heated, the gel releases water vapor, creating cooling effect. No moving parts, no noise, minimal maintenance.
