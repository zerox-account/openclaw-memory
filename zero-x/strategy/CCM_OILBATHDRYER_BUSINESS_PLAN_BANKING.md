## 1. TECHNOLOGY DEEP DIVE

### 1.1 Core Technology Explanation

**What is CCM OilBathDryer?**

CCM (Continuous Contact Matter) OilBathDryer is a proprietary thermal drying system that uses heat transfer oil as the thermal medium to remove moisture from sewage sludge. Unlike conventional dryers that use direct heat or high-temperature air, the CCM system operates at significantly lower temperatures (120-180°C vs 400-800°C), resulting in:

- **Energy savings of 50-70%**
- **Reduced thermal degradation** of organic matter
- **Safer operation** (below auto-ignition temperature)
- **Better final product quality** (higher calorific value)

**Technical Principle:**

The system works on the principle of indirect heat transfer. Wet sludge is introduced into a heated chamber where it comes into contact with hot surfaces heated by thermal oil. The oil is heated in a separate circuit (electrically or via gasification), allowing precise temperature control and energy recovery.

```
TECHNICAL PROCESS FLOW:

Wet Sludge Input (75-85% moisture)
    ↓
Feed System (metered delivery)
    ↓
CCM Drying Chamber (120-180°C)
    ├── Heat Transfer Oil Circuit
    ├── Moisture Evaporation
    └── Vapor Recovery
    ↓
Dried Product Output (10-15% moisture)
    ↓
┌─────────┬──────────┬─────────┐
↓         ↓          ↓         ↓
Biochar   Thermal    Potential Energy
(Fuel)    Energy     Recovery via
          (Reuse)    X-150 System
```
