## 1. HEATPIPE REFORMER (HPR) TECHNOLOGY PATENTS

### 1.1 Background on Patent Search

The Heatpipe Reformer (HPR) technology was developed based on research by Prof. Dr. Jürgen Karl at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU). The technology uses an innovative allothermal gasification process based on heat pipes to convert biomass into high-quality synthesis gas with significantly higher efficiency than conventional gasification methods.

## 2. ENTRADE E3/E5 OPERATIONAL SITES

### 2.1 Technology Overview

The Entrade E3 and E5 units are compact biomass gasification systems based on the Heatpipe Reformer technology:

| Model | Electrical Output | Fuel Type | Application |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Entrade E3** | ~3 kW (3-5 kW range) | Wood pellets, biomass | Residential/Small commercial |
| **Entrade E5** | ~5 kW (5-10 kW range) | Wood pellets, biomass | Commercial/Industrial micro-CHP |

**Key Features:**
- Allothermal gasification using heat pipe heat transfer
- High cold gas efficiency (~75-80%)
- Low tar content in product gas
- Modular, containerized design
- Suitable for distributed energy generation
