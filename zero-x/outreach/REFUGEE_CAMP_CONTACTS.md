## Pitch Strategy: X-150 for Refugee Camps

### Core Value Proposition

> **"Base-load power + waste management + job creation for refugee camps — all in one containerized unit."**

### Key Benefits

| Benefit | Refugee Impact | Humanitarian Agency Impact |
|---------|---------------|---------------------------|
| **24/7 Electricity** | Safety, education, connectivity | Reduced diesel generator costs |
| **Waste Processing** | Cleaner camps, less disease | Landfill diversion, compliance |
| **Job Creation** | 5-10 jobs per unit (refugees + host) | Economic integration, dignity |
| **Multi-Output** | Power, cooling (food preservation), heat | Flexibility for camp needs |
| **Rapid Deployment** | 6-7 weeks vs. 2+ years | Faster response, lower risk |

### Sample Pitch

> **Subject:** Waste-to-Energy Mini-Grids for Kakuma — Zero-X Pilot Proposal
>
> Dear Andrea,
>
> The X-150 unit converts camp organic waste (food scraps, agricultural residue) into 650kW of base-load electricity — enough for 15,000 refugees.
>
> **Kakuma pilot concept:**
> - 2 units serving 30,000 refugees in Kakuma 1 or 2
> - Refugee-operated: 10 jobs (feedstock collection, operation, maintenance)
> - Waste-to-cooling: Cold storage for WFP food distribution
> - Co-designed with refugee community (not just for them)
>
> **Cost savings:** Replace diesel generators ($0.35/kWh) with waste-to-energy ($0.12/kWh)
>
> **Validation:** Project Cometha (Paris) — 116+ hours operation, Fraunhofer certified
>
> Would NRC Kenya be open to a site assessment and co-design workshop?
