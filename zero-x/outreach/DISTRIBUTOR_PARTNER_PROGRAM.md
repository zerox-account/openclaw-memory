# Zero-X Global Distributor Partner Program

**Strategy:** Build local capability through channel partners  
**Value Prop:** "Deploy X-150 in your market — we provide technology, you provide local presence"  
**Target:** 5-10 anchor distributors covering 20+ countries by Year 2  
**Status:** Framework Design

---

## Why Distributors (vs Direct Sales)

| Challenge | Direct Sales | Distributor Model |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Local relationships** | Build from scratch | Leverage existing trust |
| **Installation/service** | Fly in technicians | Local trained partners |
| **Regulatory/navigating** | Learn each market | Distributor has permits/licences |
| **Language/culture** | Hire locally | Native capability |
| **Working capital** | Zero-X carries inventory | Distributor buys units |
| **Sales cycle** | 12-18 months | 6-9 months (local credibility) |

**Trade-off:** Lower margin per unit (30-40% vs 50%+) → Higher volume, faster scale

| Criteria | Role |
|----------|------|

## Distributor Recruitment Playbook

### Step 1: Identify (Week 1-2)

**Sources:**
- 100-contact database (prioritize equipment sellers, EPCs)
- Trade show attendee lists (Power-Gen Africa, Intersolar)
- Industry associations (ARE, AMDA, solar associations)
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator ("diesel generator distributor" + geography)
- Competitor channel partners (Husk Power, Powerhive distributors)

**Target List:** 20 prospects per Tier 1 market = 80 initial targets

### Step 2: Qualify (Week 2-3)

**Qualification Criteria Scorecard:**

| Criteria | Weight | Score (1-5) |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Technical capability | 25% | |
| Financial strength | 20% | |
| Market access/customer base | 20% | |
| Cultural fit/commitment | 20% | |
| Regulatory relationships | 15% | |
| **Total** | **100%** | **Minimum 3.5/5 to proceed** |

**Discovery Questions:**
1. "What's your current product mix? What's missing?"
2. "Who are your top 10 customers? Any waste/energy overlap?"
3. "How do you handle installation and service?"
4. "What's your typical project size and sales cycle?"
5. "Are you looking to add new product lines this year?"

### Step 3: Validate (Week 3-4)

**Technical Validation:**
- Invite to Brandenburg/Zittau for demo
- Share Cometha data, Fraunhofer validation
- Virtual site visit (Paris or Germany)

**Commercial Validation:**
- NDA signed
- Financial references checked
- Credit check (Dun & Bradstreet or local equivalent)

### Step 4: Sign (Week 4-6)

**Distributor Agreement Key Terms:**

| Term | Standard | Negotiable Range |
|------|----------|------------------|
| **Territory** | Exclusive by country/region | County-level exclusivity |
| **Minimum order** | 2 units/year | 1-5 units based on market size |
| **Discount** | 30% off list | 25-40% based on volume |
| **Payment terms** | 50% order, 50% delivery | Letter of credit for large orders |
| **Marketing commitment** | €10K/year local marketing | Co-marketing fund matching |
| **Training** | 2 weeks in Germany | On-site training for large orders |
| **Support** | Remote + spare parts | Local technician embedding |
| **Term** | 2 years with renewal | Annual performance review |

### Step 5: Enable (Week 6-12)

**90-Day Launch Program:**

| Week | Activity | Zero-X Support |
|------|----------|----------------|
| 1-2 | Training (technical, sales, service) | Fly to Germany or on-site |
| 3-4 | Marketing materials localization | Provide templates, fund translation |
| 5-6 | First joint customer meetings | Zero-X technical lead attends |
| 7-8 | Pilot site identification | Support proposal development |
| 9-10 | First order closing | Executive involvement |
| 11-12 | Installation support | Technician on-site for first unit |

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Distributor underperformance** | Annual review, performance improvement plan, termination clause |
| **Distributor financial failure** | Credit insurance, milestone payments, letter of credit |
| **Technical failures damage brand** | Strict installation certification, remote monitoring, rapid response SLA |
| **Distributor sells competing product** | Non-compete clause, performance-based exclusivity |
| **Intellectual property leakage** | NDA, limited technical disclosure, component black-boxing |
| **Regulatory changes** | Distributor responsible for permits, Zero-X provides documentation |

## Financial Model: Distributor vs Direct

### Unit Economics Comparison

| Metric | Direct Sales | Distributor Sales |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| **List price** | €300K | €300K |
| **Discount to distributor** | — | 30% (€90K) |
| **Zero-X revenue** | €300K | €210K |
| **Sales cost** | €45K (travel, demos, cycles) | €15K (support, training) |
| **Gross margin** | €255K (85%) | €195K (93% on lower revenue) |
| **Volume multiplier** | 1x | 3-5x (distributor scale) |
| **Effective gross profit** | €255K/unit | €585K-975K (3-5 units) |

**Break-even:** Distributor model wins at >2 units/year per distributor
