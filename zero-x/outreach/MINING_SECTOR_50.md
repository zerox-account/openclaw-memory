## Market Opportunity Summary

**Africa Diesel Generator Market:** $6.8B (2024) → $9.6B (2030)

**Mining Sector Profile:**
- Remote mines spend $0.30-0.50/kWh on diesel vs. $0.05-0.10 for grid
- Mining sector uses 1000-2000+ kVA generators as primary power
- Diesel = 30-50% of mining operating costs
- 600M+ people in Sub-Saharan Africa lack electricity — mining is major diesel user

**Key Value Propositions:**
1. Replace expensive diesel with waste-to-power (fuel cost savings)
2. Reduce carbon footprint, earn carbon credits (ESG compliance)
3. Turn mining waste into energy (circular economy)
4. 150kW electric + 300kW thermal + biochar fertilizer (multiple outputs)
5. Containerized, deployable to remote sites (mining camp compatible)

**Best Approach:**
- Lead with cost savings — Diesel is 30-50% of operating costs
- Emphasize ESG/carbon credits — Mining companies under decarbonization pressure
- Offer pilot programs — Start with one site, scale to operations
- Partner with existing generator suppliers — Danmik, Generex, HIMOINSA
- Target sustainability officers — They drive ESG initiatives

## Key Messaging Framework for Mining Sector

### Primary Messages

**For Energy Managers (Operations Focus):**
> "Replace expensive diesel with waste-to-power. Cut operating costs 30-50% with containerized deployment to remote sites."

**For Sustainability Officers (ESG Focus):**
> "Reduce Scope 1 emissions, earn carbon credits, and achieve decarbonization targets with circular economy energy."

**For CEOs/CFOs (Financial Focus):**
> "Turn your camp waste into revenue. 150kW power + 300kW thermal + biochar fertilizer. Diesel costs eliminated, fertilizer sold."

**For Procurement (Supplier Focus):**
> "Containerized, 6-7 week deployment. Proven technology (Project Cometha). Local jobs created."

### Value Proposition by Use Case

**Mining Camps:**
- 24/7 power for accommodation, offices, processing
- Thermal for heating, hot water, kitchen operations
- Biochar for site remediation/revegetation

**Remote Processing:**
- Power for crushing, grinding, sorting equipment
- Thermal for process heat requirements
- Distilled water for industrial processes

**Exploration Sites:**
- Temporary but long-term deployment (months-years)
- Fuel independence from supply chain disruptions
- Rapid redeployment as operations move
