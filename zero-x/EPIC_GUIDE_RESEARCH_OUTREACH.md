## I. THE GOLDEN RULES

### Rule #1: Intent Before Product
**ALWAYS** start with what you seek, not what you offer.

❌ WRONG: "We have developed the X-150 system..."
✅ RIGHT: "We seek research partners for collaboration in thermochemical waste conversion..."

**Why:** Researchers are inundated with sales pitches. Leading with intent signals you're a peer, not a vendor.

### Rule #2: One Perfect Technical Sentence
The X-150 description must be exact, every time:

> "Das X-150 ist ein containerisiertes, einstufiges Downdraft-Vergasungssystem mit integriertem Teer-Reformer, optimiert für aschelastige Multifuel-Betriebe und flexibel für KWK-Betrieb mit Luft oder Oxy-Steam-Prozess zur Syngas-Erzeugung für direkte Nutzung oder Pipeline-Einspeisung."

**Non-negotiable elements:**
- Containerized (containerisiertes)
- Single-stage (einstufiges)
- Downdraft (Downdraft)
- Tar reformer (Teer-Reformer)
- High ash (aschelastige)
- Multi-fuel (Multifuel)
- CHP (KWK)
- Air OR oxy-steam (Luft oder Oxy-Steam)
- Syngas (Syngas)

### Rule #3: Facts Are Immutable
Cométha facts never change:
- **Funders:** Syctom/SIAAP (Paris) – NEVER CEAP/CCAF
- **Duration:** 1,000+ operating hours
- **Validation:** Fraunhofer Institute + Hochschule Zittau-Görlitz
- **Professor:** Prof. Dr. Dr. Tobias Zschunke (NOT Trunke, NOT André)
- **Scale:** 15 kg/h pilot

### Rule #4: The CTA Format
**ALWAYS** use this exact structure:

> "Wären Sie an einem fachlichen Austausch über [SPECIFIC TOPIC] interessiert?"

**Why:** "Wären Sie..." is conditional-polite (PhD-level German). "Haben Sie Interesse..." sounds salesy.

## III. CONTACT DATA VALIDATION (100% Accuracy Protocol)

### Step 1: Multi-Source Verification
**NEVER rely on one source.** Cross-reference minimum 3 sources:

1. Institutional website (imprint/contact)
2. Research publications (author affiliations)
3. ORCID/ResearchGate profiles
4. Conference proceedings
5. Verified via phone (if critical)

### Step 2: Email Format Verification
**Academic institutions typically use:**
- firstname.lastname@institution.de
- f.lastname@institution.de
- lastname@institution.de

**ALWAYS verify:**
- Middle initials (Dr. vs. Dr.-Ing.)
- Current position (professors move!)
- Department accuracy

### Step 3: The Validation Checklist
Before sending:
□ Is the person still at this institution?
□ Is the email format correct?
□ Is the title accurate (Prof./Dr./Dr.-Ing.)?
□ Have you spelled the name correctly?
□ Is this the right department?
□ Is there a more specific contact?

### Step 4: Red Flags
**STOP if you find:**
- Obsolete profile (last updated 3+ years ago)
- Generic info@ address for senior researcher
- Conflicting information across sources
- "Former employee" mentions
- Deceased person (like Prof. Kau DLR)

## V. ARTICULATING YOUR INTENT

### The Partnership Framework
**ALWAYS frame as:**
- Seeking collaboration ("Forschungspartner")
- Building peer networks ("Netzwerk von Peers")
- Academic exchange ("fachlicher Austausch")
- Joint applications ("gemeinsame Förderanträge")

**NEVER frame as:**
- Selling technology
- Offering services
- Seeking customers
- Data delivery

### The Win-Win Language

✅ **Use:**
- "Gemeinsame Publikationen" (joint publications)
- "Beiderseitiger Nutzen" (mutual benefit)
- "Austausch auf Augenhöhe" (exchange as equals)
- "Erweitern Ihre Forschung" (extend your research)

❌ **Avoid:**
- "Ihr Mehrwert" (your added value)
- "Wir bieten" (we offer)
- "Unsere Lösung" (our solution)
- "Profitieren Sie" (you benefit)

### Financial Information (NEVER Include)
- Revenue figures
- Investment amounts
- Cost per unit
- Burn rate
- Grant amounts
- Valuation
- **Rule:** If it has €, $, or %, leave it out unless explicitly approved

### Sales Language (NEVER Use)
- "Ihr Mehrwert"
- "Wir bieten"
- "Unsere Lösung"
- "Profitieren Sie"
- "Kosten sparend"
- "Effizienzsteigerung"

### Casual Language (NEVER Use)
- "Hey" / "Hi"
- "Interesse an einem Kaffee?"
- "Schnell mal"
- "Einfach"
- "Cool"

### Assumptions (NEVER Make)
- Project involvement without verification
- Current positions (people move!)
- Research interests (check recent pubs!)
- Availability
- Decision-making authority

### Formatting (NEVER Do)
- HTML emails (plain text only)
- Attachments in first email
- More than 200 words
- Multiple CTAs
- Generic copy-paste without personalization

## XII. CRISIS MANAGEMENT (When Things Go Wrong)

### Scenario 1: Wrong Name Used
**Action:** Immediate correction email
> "Sehr geehrter Herr [Correct Name], in meiner vorherigen Nachricht habe ich leider einen Fehler bei der Anrede gemacht. Es tut mir sehr leid..."

### Scenario 2: Wrong Institution Referenced
**Action:** Acknowledge, apologize, move on
> "Ich habe fälschlicherweise [wrong project] erwähnt. Korrekt ist [right project]..."

### Scenario 3: Email Bounced
**Action:** Search alternative contact method
- Call institution
- Use general info@ address
- Check ResearchGate
- LinkedIn message

### Scenario 4: No Response After 2 Weeks
**Action:** Polite follow-up
> "Ich wollte kurz nachfragen, ob meine Nachricht vom [date] angekommen ist..."

### Scenario 5: Negative Response
**Action:** Professional acknowledgment
> "Vielen Dank für Ihre Rückmeldung. Ich verstehe, dass derzeit keine Kooperation möglich ist..."
