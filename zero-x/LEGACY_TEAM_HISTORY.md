- **Origin:** German startup from Technical University of Munich
- **Technology:** Heatpipe Reformer (HPR) - high-efficiency biomass gasification

- **Focus:** Development of core HPR technology

### 2. Entrade Energiesysteme AG (Founded 2009)
- **Leader:** Julien Uhlig
- **Acquisition:** May 2013 - Acquired Agnion Energy to integrate HPR
- **Products:** E3, E4, E5 modular small-scale power plants
- **Focus:** Manufacturing and deployment

### 3. Arensis (Founded ~2016)
- **Location:** US-based (Los Angeles)
- **Affiliation:** Los Angeles Cleantech Incubator (LACI) portfolio
- **Role:** International deployment and project financing arm
- **Focus:** Global expansion, UK and Japan markets

## TECHNOLOGY: HEATPIPE REFORMER (HPR)

### Core Innovation
- Allothermal steam gasification process
- Indirectly heated (heat pipes transfer energy)
- High efficiency biomass-to-energy conversion
- Modular, containerized design (20-foot shipping containers)

### Technical Publications
1. "The agnion Heatpipe-Reformer—operating experiences and evaluation..." (Biomass Conversion and Biorefinery, 2013)
   - First 500 kW pilot plant results
   
2. "Biomass heat pipe reformer – Design and performance of an indirectly heated steam gasifier" (Fuel, 2013)
   - Foundational paper on allothermal process
   
3. "Performance and Efficiency of the Heatpipe Reformer Gasification System" (ResearchGate, 2017)
   - 100 kW pilot stand evaluation
   
4. "Combining Heatpipe Reformer technology with hydrogen-intensified methanation" (Applied Energy, 2018)
   - Synthetic natural gas (SNG) production
