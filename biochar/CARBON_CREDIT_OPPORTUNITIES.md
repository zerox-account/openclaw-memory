## 1. Carbon Credit Fundamentals

### 1.1 How Biochar Carbon Credits Work

**The Carbon Removal Process:**

1. **Biomass Growth**: Plants capture CO₂ from atmosphere
2. **Pyrolysis**: Biomass heated without oxygen
3. **Carbon Stabilization**: Carbon converted to stable aromatic structures
4. **Application**: Biochar applied to soil or products
5. **Permanence**: Carbon remains sequestered for 100+ years

**Carbon Accounting:**
```
Carbon Credits = (Biochar Carbon Content × Biochar Mass) - (Production Emissions)

Example:
- 1 ton biochar
- 40% carbon content
- = 0.4 tons carbon
- = 1.47 tons CO₂ equivalent (×3.67 molecular weight ratio)
- Minus production emissions (~0.2 tons CO₂)
- = ~1.2-1.5 CORCs per ton biochar
```

### 1.2 Why Biochar Commands Premium Prices

| Feature | Biochar | Forest Credits | Direct Air Capture |
|---------|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Permanence** | 100+ years | 30-50 years | 10,000+ years |
| **Co-benefits** | Soil health, water | Biodiversity | None |
| **Maturity** | Proven at scale | Mature | Early stage |
| **Cost** | €50-200/tCO₂ | €10-30/tCO₂ | €400-800/tCO₂ |
| **Additionality** | High | Variable | High |
| **Verification** | Straightforward | Complex | Complex |

**Market Position:** Biochar offers the best combination of permanence, co-benefits, and cost-effectiveness.

### 2.2 Puro.earth Biochar Methodology

**Version:** Edition 2025 (latest update)

**Core Requirements:**

1. **Sustainable Feedstock**
   - Waste biomass preferred (additionality)
   - No competition with food production
   - No deforestation
   - Chain of custody documentation

2. **Pyrolysis Requirements**
   - Minimum temperature: 350°C
   - Oxygen-limited environment
   - Emissions control

3. **Carbon Quantification**
   - Laboratory carbon content analysis
   - Mass balance calculations
   - Production emission deductions
   - Uncertainty discounts (typically 10-20%)

4. **Application Requirements**
   - Soil application (agriculture, forestry)
   - Material incorporation (concrete, asphalt)
   - Documentation of end use
   - Prevention of combustion/oxidation

5. **Monitoring & Verification**
   - Annual third-party audits
   - Batch tracking
   - Application verification
   - MRV (Measurement, Reporting, Verification) system

## 5. Alternative Carbon Markets

### 5.1 EU Carbon Removal Certification Framework (CRCF)

**Status:** Launched 2024-2025

**Overview:**
- EU-regulated carbon removal certification
- Creates "Certified Carbon Removal Units" (CCRUs)
- Aligns with EU climate targets
- Expected to become the standard for EU-based projects

**Requirements:**
- QU.A.L.ITY criteria (Quantification, Additionality, Long-term storage, Sustainability)
- Third-party verification
- EU-approved methodologies
- Registry integration

**Pricing Expectations:**
- €80-150/CCRU initially
- Potential premium over voluntary markets
- Compliance market linkage possible

**Zero-X Relevance:**
- German location = EU project
- Early adoption advantage
- Future-proofing against regulatory changes

### 5.2 Nori

**Overview:**
- US-focused carbon removal marketplace
- Simpler methodology than Puro.earth
- Lower prices but easier entry

**Pricing:** $15-25/NRT (Nori Removal Ton)

**Requirements:**
- US-based projects preferred
- Less stringent verification
- Annual verification

**Zero-X Relevance:**
- Limited (EU project)
- Possible for US exports

### 5.3 Gold Standard

**Overview:**
- Established carbon credit standard
- Focus on sustainable development co-benefits
- Biochar methodology in development

**Pricing:** $10-30/tCO₂

**Requirements:**
- Demonstrate SDG contributions
- Stakeholder engagement
- Third-party verification

**Zero-X Relevance:**
- Possible additional revenue stream
- Lower prices than Puro.earth
- Good for corporate storytelling

### 5.4 Verra VCS

**Overview:**
- Largest voluntary carbon market
- Biochar methodology under development
- Wide buyer network

**Pricing:** $5-15/tCO₂ (expected for biochar)

**Zero-X Relevance:**
- Future option
- Lower prices
- High liquidity

## 6. Sales Strategies

### 6.1 Direct to Puro.earth Marketplace

**Process:**
1. List CORCs on Puro.earth registry
2. Buyers purchase directly
3. Puro.earth handles settlement
4. Revenue minus 10-15% platform fee

**Advantages:**
- Access to global buyer pool
- Transparent pricing
- Simplified sales process

**Disadvantages:**
- Platform fees
- Price competition
- Less control over buyers

### 6.2 Long-Term Offtake Agreements

**Structure:**
- 3-10 year purchase agreements
- Fixed or escalating prices
- Annual delivery commitments
- Credit-worthy buyers

**Advantages:**
- Revenue certainty
- Project finance enablement
- Price premiums for certainty

**Disadvantages:**
- Less upside if prices rise
- Delivery obligations
- Counterparty risk

**Target Buyers for Offtakes:**
- Microsoft Sustainability Fund
- Stripe Climate
- Shopify Sustainability Fund
- Corporate venture arms

### 6.3 Forward Sales

**Structure:**
- Sell future CORC production
- Delivery in 1-3 years
- Prepayment or milestone payments

**Advantages:**
- Early revenue
- Project development funding
- Price locking

**Disadvantages:**
- Execution risk
- Price may be lower than spot
