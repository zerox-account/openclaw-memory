**Document Version:** 1.0

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the step-by-step process for achieving European Biochar Certificate (EBC) certification for biochar produced from the X-150 gasifier using digester sludge feedstock. EBC certification is the gold standard for biochar quality in Europe and is essential for accessing premium agricultural markets and carbon credit programs.

**Target Certification Level:** EBC-Basic (achievable with sludge-derived biochar)  
**Timeline:** 3-6 months from initiation  
**Estimated Cost:** €8,000-15,000  
**Certification Body:** Carbon Standards International

---

## 1. Understanding EBC Certification Classes

### 1.1 EBC-Basic (Zero-X Target)

**Scope:** Non-food agricultural applications

**Permitted Uses:**
- Soil amendment for non-food crops (energy crops, ornamental plants)
- Composting additive
- Substrate for tree nurseries (non-food)
- Manure treatment and stable bedding
- Silage additive
- Industrial applications

**Requirements:**
- Basic feedstock documentation
- Production temperature verification (>350°C)
- Heavy metals testing (6 elements)
- PAH testing (16 compounds)
- Annual third-party audit

### 1.2 EBC-Agro (Stretch Goal)

**Scope:** Food agriculture applications

**Additional Requirements:**
- Stricter heavy metals limits
- Full toxicant assessment (PAHs, dioxins, PCBs)
- H:C ratio <0.7
- More extensive documentation

**Zero-X Feasibility:** Possible if:
- Heavy metals are below thresholds
- Consider feedstock blending
- Additional processing if needed

### 1.3 EBC-AgroOrganic (Not Applicable)

**Scope:** Organic farming

**Requirement:** 100% organic feedstock

**Zero-X Status:** Digester sludge not eligible

### 1.4 EBC-Consumer (Not Applicable)

**Scope:** Direct consumer products

**Requirement:** Highest purity standards

**Zero-X Status:** Sludge biochar unlikely to qualify

## 4. Phase 2: Testing & Documentation (Month 2-3)

### 4.1 Required Testing for EBC-Basic

#### 4.1.1 Heavy Metals Analysis

**Elements to Test:**
- Cadmium (Cd)
- Lead (Pb)
- Mercury (Hg)
- Chromium (Cr)
- Copper (Cu)
- Zinc (Zn)
- Nickel (Ni)

**EBC-Basic Thresholds:**

| Element | Limit (mg/kg dry matter) |
|---------|-------------------------|
| Cadmium | 1.5 |
| Lead | 150 |
| Mercury | 1.0 |
| Chromium | 100 |
| Copper | 300 |
| Zinc | 800 |
| Nickel | 50 |

**Zero-X Consideration:**
Digester sludge may have elevated heavy metals depending on wastewater source. Industrial wastewater inputs are higher risk than municipal.

#### 4.1.2 PAH (Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons) Testing

**16 EPA Priority PAHs:**
- Naphthalene
- Acenaphthylene
- Acenaphthene
- Fluorene
- Phenanthrene
- Anthracene
- Fluoranthene
- Pyrene
- Benzo[a]anthracene
- Chrysene
- Benzo[b]fluoranthene
- Benzo[k]fluoranthene
- Benzo[a]pyrene
- Indeno[1,2,3-cd]pyrene
- Dibenz[a,h]anthracene
- Benzo[g,h,i]perylene

**EBC-Basic Threshold:**
- Sum of 16 PAHs: <12 mg/kg dry matter
- Benzo[a]pyrene: <0.7 mg/kg dry matter

**Zero-X Advantage:**
Gasification at high temperatures typically results in low PAH content (complete combustion of volatiles).

#### 4.1.3 Additional Basic Parameters

| Parameter | Method | Purpose |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Moisture Content | EN 18134 | Standardization |
| pH | EN 13037 | Quality indicator |
| Electrical Conductivity | EN 13038 | Salinity assessment |
| Dry Matter Content | EN 18134 | Basis for calculations |

### 4.2 Sampling Protocol

**Composite Sample Requirements:**

1. **Sample Collection:**
   - Collect 10-20 sub-samples from different batches
   - Minimum 1 kg total sample
   - Use clean, dry containers
   - Avoid contamination

2. **Sample Preparation:**
   - Air dry or oven dry at 40°C
   - Grind to <2mm particle size
   - Homogenize thoroughly
   - Split into three portions:
     - Primary analysis
     - Backup/reference
     - Archive (6 months minimum)

3. **Documentation:**
   - Sample ID and date
   - Production batch numbers
   - Feedstock source for each batch
   - Storage conditions

### 4.3 Documentation Package

**Required Documents for Application:**

1. **Company Information**
   - Business registration
   - Production facility address
   - Contact details

2. **Feedstock Documentation**
   - Source description
   - Supply agreements
   - Quality control procedures

3. **Production Documentation**
   - Equipment specifications
   - Process parameters
   - Temperature records
   - Batch records template

4. **Quality Management**
   - Testing protocols
   - Sampling procedures
   - Record keeping system
   - Traceability system

5. **Environmental Compliance**
   - Emissions permits
   - Waste handling permits
   - Environmental impact assessment (if required)

## 5. Phase 3: Application Submission (Month 3-4)

### 5.1 Application Process

**Step 1: Pre-Application Consultation**
- Contact Carbon Standards International
- Discuss feedstock and production specifics
- Confirm certification pathway
- Cost: Free consultation

**Step 2: Complete Application Form**
- Download from carbon-standards.com
- Provide all company and facility details
- Submit preliminary documentation

**Step 3: Pay Application Fee**
- EBC-Basic: ~€2,000-3,000 (initial)
- Annual fee: ~€1,000-1,500

**Step 4: Submit Documentation Package**
- All documents from Phase 2
- Laboratory test results
- Production records (minimum 3 months)

### 5.2 Application Review

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks

**Possible Outcomes:**
1. **Approved for Audit** → Proceed to Phase 4
2. **Additional Information Required** → Provide missing documents
3. **Not Approved** → Address issues and reapply

---

## 6. Phase 4: Audit & Verification (Month 4-5)

### 6.1 Third-Party Audit

**Audit Components:**

1. **Document Review**
   - Feedstock records
   - Production logs
   - Testing results
   - Quality procedures

2. **Facility Inspection**
   - Production equipment
   - Storage facilities
   - Sampling procedures
   - Record keeping systems

3. **Interview**
   - Production staff
   - Quality manager
   - Management representative

**Audit Duration:** 1-2 days on-site

**Audit Cost:** Included in certification fee

### 6.2 Corrective Actions

If non-conformities are identified:

1. **Minor Non-Conformities:**
   - 30 days to correct
   - Evidence submission
   - No re-audit required

2. **Major Non-Conformities:**
   - 60 days to correct
   - May require follow-up audit
   - Additional costs may apply

## 7. Phase 5: Certification Issued (Month 5-6)

### 7.1 Certificate Contents

**EBC Certificate Includes:**

- Certified biochar classes (EBC-Basic)
- Permitted applications
- Certificate number
- Issue and expiry dates
- Audit body

### 7.2 Certificate Use

**Permitted Claims:**
- "EBC-Basic Certified"
- Use of EBC logo (with approval)
- Reference to certificate number

**Labeling Requirements:**
- EBC logo
- Certificate number
- Batch number
- Production date
- Application restrictions

### 7.3 Certificate Validity

- **Initial Validity:** 1 year
- **Renewal:** Annual audits required
- **Suspension:** Possible if non-conformities not addressed
- **Withdrawal:** For serious violations

## 9. Zero-X Specific Considerations

### 9.1 High Ash Content Strategy

**Challenge:** Digester sludge biochar typically has 30-50% ash content

**EBC-Basic Status:** Permitted (no ash limit for Basic class)

**Market Implications:**
- ✅ EBC-Basic certification achievable
- ⚠️ May limit agricultural applications
- ✅ Strong fit for industrial applications
- ✅ Carbon credits unaffected by ash content

**Recommended Positioning:**
- "EBC-Basic Certified Biochar from Circular Feedstock"
- Emphasize waste valorization and carbon removal
- Target industrial and non-food agricultural markets

### 9.2 Heavy Metals Risk Assessment

**Risk Factors:**
- Industrial wastewater in digester feed
- Geographic location (industrial areas)
- Age of wastewater treatment infrastructure

**Mitigation Strategies:**
1. Source sludge from municipal (not industrial) WWTPs
2. Request upstream heavy metals data
3. Pre-screen feedstock batches
4. Consider blending with lower-ash feedstocks

### 9.3 Production Optimization

**Recommendations for X-150:**

1. **Temperature Monitoring:**
   - Install continuous temperature logging
   - Ensure >350°C minimum in reaction zone
   - Document temperature profiles

2. **Batch Consistency:**
   - Standardize feedstock preparation
   - Control moisture content
   - Maintain consistent feed rates

3. **Quality Control:**
   - Implement in-house testing (pH, moisture)
   - Regular laboratory verification
   - Batch-to-batch comparison
