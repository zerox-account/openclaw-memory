## 2. Production Technologies & Zero-X Positioning

### 2.1 Pyrolysis Methods Comparison

| Method | Temperature | Residence Time | Biochar Yield | Energy Output | Suitability for Zero-X |
|--------|-------------|----------------|---------------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Slow Pyrolysis** | 350-550°C | Hours-days | 25-35% | Low heat | ✅ **Current X-150** |
| **Fast Pyrolysis** | 500-700°C | Seconds | 12-20% | Bio-oil focus | ❌ Not suitable |
| **Flash Pyrolysis** | >700°C | <1 second | 10-15% | Maximum liquid | ❌ Not suitable |
| **Gasification** | 700-1000°C | Minutes | 5-10% | Syngas focus | ⚠️ Partial match |

**Zero-X X-150 Analysis:**
- The X-150 operates as a gasifier with biochar as co-product
- High-ash biochar from digester sludge feedstock
- Typical yield: 5-15% biochar by weight
- **Advantage**: Energy generation + biochar production
- **Challenge**: High ash content may limit agricultural applications

### 2.2 Feedstock Considerations

**Zero-X Feedstock: Digester Sludge**
- **Pros**: Consistent supply, waste valorization, carbon-negative
- **Cons**: High ash content (30-50%), potential contaminants
- **Best Applications**: Construction materials, water filtration, industrial uses
- **Limited Applications**: Premium agriculture (EBC-Basic only)

## 3. Quality Parameters & Standards

### 3.1 Key Quality Metrics

| Parameter | Premium Grade | Standard Grade | Zero-X Sludge Biochar |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **H:C Ratio** | <0.4 | <0.7 | Likely 0.5-0.8 |
| **O:C Ratio** | <0.1 | <0.2 | Likely 0.15-0.3 |
| **Surface Area** | >500 m²/g | >200 m²/g | Likely 100-300 m²/g |
| **Ash Content** | <10% | <20% | **30-50%** |
| **pH** | 8-10 | 7-11 | Likely 9-11 |
| **Heavy Metals** | Below thresholds | Below thresholds | Requires testing |

### 3.2 Certification Standards

#### European Biochar Certificate (EBC)

| Class | Requirements | Applications | Zero-X Feasibility |
|-------|--------------|--------------|-------------------|
| **EBC-Basic** | Minimal testing | Non-food agriculture | ✅ **Achievable** |
| **EBC-Agro** | Full testing, H:C<0.7 | Food agriculture | ⚠️ Possible with blending |
| **EBC-AgroOrganic** | Organic feedstock only | Organic farming | ❌ Not applicable |
| **EBC-Consumer** | Highest purity | Direct consumer use | ❌ Not applicable |

**Key EBC Requirements for Zero-X:**
- Feedstock documentation (digester sludge source)
- Production temperature monitoring (>350°C)
- Heavy metals testing (Cd, Pb, Hg, Cr, Cu, Zn, Ni)
- PAH (polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons) testing
- Annual third-party audit

#### International Biochar Initiative (IBI)

- Similar to EBC but US-focused
- Three classes: Class 1, 2, 3
- Requires testing for:
  - Toxicant assessment (PAHs, dioxins, PCBs)
  - Heavy metals
  - pH and EC
  - Moisture content
  - Particle size distribution

## 4. Carbon Credit Markets

### 4.1 Puro.earth CORCs

**Methodology Overview:**
- First biochar carbon crediting methodology (2019)
- Updated 2022, 2025
- Issues CO₂ Removal Certificates (CORCs)
- 100+ year permanence guarantee

**Pricing (2024-2025):**
- Spot market: €80-150/CORC
- Long-term offtake: €50-100/CORC
- Premium projects: €150-300/CORC

**Eligibility Requirements:**
1. Sustainable feedstock sourcing
2. Minimum pyrolysis temperature (350°C)
3. Third-party verification
4. MRV (Measurement, Reporting, Verification) system
5. Environmental and social safeguards

**Zero-X Considerations:**
- Digester sludge qualifies as waste feedstock (additionality)
- Energy co-generation improves project economics
- Requires Puro.earth registration and verification

### 4.2 Other Carbon Markets

| Market | Price Range | Requirements | Suitability |
|--------|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Nori** | $15-25/NRT | US-focused, simpler | ⚠️ Limited |
| **Gold Standard** | $10-30/tCO2 | Additional SDG benefits | ⚠️ Possible |
| **Verra VCS** | $5-15/tCO2 | Methodology in development | ❌ Not yet |
| **EU CRCF** | €80-120/tCO2 | EU certification | ✅ **Best fit** |

### 4.3 Carbon Credit Economics for Zero-X

**Assumptions:**
- Biochar carbon content: 30-50%
- Carbon sequestration rate: 2-3 tCO2/ton biochar
- Puro.earth price: €100/CORC

**Revenue Calculation (per ton biochar):**
- Carbon credits: 2.5 tCO2 × €100 = **€250/ton**
- Product sale (EBC-Basic): €100-200/ton
- **Total potential: €350-450/ton**
